# Voice & tone

**Voice is who the product is — constant. Tone is how it reads in a given moment — variable.** Get voice
from the project's identity; flex tone by the user's emotional state in that context.

## Deriving the voice

Pull the three personality words + register from **`ATELIER.md`** (or the Direction Doc's type voice).
Turn each trait into a concrete writing rule:

| Trait | Writing rule | Not |
|---|---|---|
| Calm | short declaratives, no exclamation, no urgency tricks | "Hurry! Don't miss out!!" |
| Expert | precise nouns, name the mechanism, no hedging | "this might possibly help maybe" |
| Warm | second person, contractions, plain words | "The user must authenticate" |
| Playful | light wit *in low-stakes spots only* (success, empty) | wit in an error or a payment screen |

Register sets the baseline: **product** (app/tool) leans efficient and quiet — the copy gets out of the
way; **brand** (marketing) can carry more personality and rhythm. One register per surface.

## Tone moves with the moment

Same voice, different tone by where the user is:

| Moment | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Success | brief, get out of the way | "Saved." |
| Error (user-fixable) | calm, specific, helpful | "That email's already in use — try signing in." |
| Error (our fault) | apologetic, reassuring, actionable | "Our fault — we kept your draft. Try again." |
| Empty / first run | encouraging, forward-looking | "Add your first project to get started." |
| Destructive confirm | serious, clear about consequence | "This permanently deletes 3 files." |
| Upsell / nudge | light, never pushy or fear-based | "Pro adds unlimited exports — upgrade any time." |

The mistake to avoid: a single flat tone everywhere (cheery in an error, or clinical in onboarding).

## Mechanics that carry voice consistently

- **Person & tense:** pick second person ("you") + active voice and hold it. Results in past tense
  ("Invite sent"); instructions in imperative ("Choose a plan").
- **Contractions** read human (use them) unless the brand is deliberately formal.
- **Sentence case** for UI by default; Title/UPPER only as a brand decision from direction.
- **Punctuation:** no exclamation marks in product UI (save for rare brand delight); no ALL-CAPS shouting;
  Oxford comma for clarity.
- **Numbers:** spell out one-nine in prose, numerals in data/UI; never invent precision.

## The buzzword denylist (say the specific thing instead)

Cut on sight in interface and marketing copy: *seamless, robust, elevate, empower, unlock, supercharge,
leverage, delve, tapestry, in today's fast-paced world, gone are the days, whether you're a…, revolutionary,
game-changing, effortless, cutting-edge, next-level, world-class.* These are filler that signals AI/template
authorship. Replace each with the concrete benefit ("syncs in under a second", not "seamless syncing").

## The read-aloud test

Read every string out loud. If it sounds like a person who knows the product talking to a person who needs
it — keep it. If it sounds like a brochure, a robot, or a wink at the reader — rewrite it plainly.
